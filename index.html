Care Options Packet

A fictional, human-reviewed mock showing how CareBridge helps a family organize the next 72 hours after a fall, hospital stay, or sudden care change.

Fictional case

Evelyn Jensen

Location
Salt Lake County, Utah
Situation
Fall at home, hospital discharge expected in 24 to 48 hours
Family ask
"We do not know what Medicare pays for or who to call first."

Safety Note

This packet is not medical, legal, or final coverage advice. If there is immediate danger, call 911. If a care recommendation, discharge order, or coverage decision is unclear, ask the discharge planner, doctor, plan, or state agency to confirm it in writing.

Step 1

What We Know

  • Evelyn lives alone and may not be safe going home without support.
  • The family needs to confirm whether the discharge plan is skilled rehab, home health, or non-medical personal care.
  • The family does not yet know whether Evelyn has Medicaid, long-term care insurance, or VA-related benefits.
  • The decision window is short, so calls should start today.
Step 2

Coverage Questions

Medicare.gov says Medicare does not pay for long-term care, including most help with bathing, dressing, transportation, meals, and other daily personal tasks.

  • Was Evelyn admitted as an inpatient or under observation?
  • Is the discharge recommendation skilled nursing, home health, rehab, hospice, or custodial care?
  • What services is the doctor ordering after discharge?
  • Does her plan require prior authorization or use a specific provider network?
  • If this becomes long-term care, should the family check Medicaid, VA, or private-pay options?
Step 3

Local Care Map

Eldercare Locator Find local aging services and Area Agency on Aging support. Utah Aging and Adult Services State aging programs, caregiver support, SHIP, ombudsman, and local services. Medicare Care Compare Compare Medicare-certified nursing homes and home health providers. Utah Medicaid Long-Term Care Check Medicaid long-term care and waiver paths.
Step 4

Cost Picture

CareScout's 2025 survey reports a national median assisted living cost of $6,200 per month. Utah will vary by city, care level, provider availability, and assessment.

Path What to check
Short-term skilled care Hospital status, doctor order, plan rules, provider network
Home support Home health eligibility vs private non-medical care
Assisted living Base rent, care-level fees, move-in fees, Medicaid policy
Medicaid or waiver Financial eligibility, care level, waitlists, local AAA referral

Next 72 hours

Family Task Board

  1. Today Call discharge planner. Ask for the written discharge recommendation, inpatient status, and what level of care is being ordered.
  2. Next 24 hours Call three realistic providers. Confirm availability, payer types, care limits, and what would make Evelyn not a fit.
  3. Next 48 hours Gather Medicare card, plan card, medication list, power of attorney documents, income/resource basics, and current doctors.
  4. This week Tour or interview the top options, check local aging services, and decide whether home with support is realistic.

Call Script: Discharge Planner

"My mother is being discharged soon and our family needs to understand the care plan. Can you confirm whether the recommendation is skilled rehab, home health, or non-medical personal care? We also need to know what is being ordered, what Medicare or her plan may review, and what we need to arrange before discharge."

Call Script: Care Provider

"We are helping our mother after a fall and need to know whether your team can support someone who may need help with walking, bathing, meals, and reminders. Do you have availability this week, what payer types do you accept, and what costs should we expect beyond the base rate?"

Agent-first operating model

Agent Fleet Workflow

CareBridge is designed as a small operating desk. Each agent owns a bounded job, then a human reviewer checks the packet before the family receives it.

1

Intake Agent

Collects the minimum facts and creates the case brief.

2

Coverage Agent

Explains Medicare questions and payer uncertainty in plain English.

3

Local Care Map Agent

Finds local resources, agencies, and provider categories to call.

4

Cost Agent

Builds rough cost ranges and benefit paths to verify.

5

Outreach Agent

Writes call scripts, emails, and provider question sets.

6

Task Board Agent

Turns the case into a 72-hour family action plan.

7

Review Agent

Flags unsupported claims, privacy risk, and advice that needs human judgment.
